About Me

I am a Cleveland native, whose heart lead them to Chicago. I show extreme enthusiasm about things I'm passionate about, and I'm drawn to others who do the same thing. I'm an ace at trivia, and hate cooking, but love cooking shows.

I met my wife in March of our senior year of college, and we wed six years later. Our courtship was one of the most difficult experiences of my life, but also the most rewarding. We both share a passion for live music, and have been to just about every music venue in the city. I play softball with friends in the summer, and gather with friends to watch Ohio State Buckeye football every fall saturday. I'm an avid supporter of the great Aston Villa Football Club, and if I’m spending time at home, I’m generally engaging in the latest single-player video game experience.

What Lead Me to Software Development

What wasn’t mentioned previously is my fascination with technology. Coming of age and hearing about the dot com era of the late 90s, I was fascinated in watching the way consumer technology developed, and remember getting an iPod with the click wheel. As I discovered how immersive the internet became, my interests developed into understanding how to code, but I ventured towards business in my first year of college.

I’ve spent six years in sales, and while I’ve developed significant skills and friendships, it has never been a source of fulfillment, and I began gravitating back towards programming. After spending time working through online tutorials, I seriously considered a bootcamp, and applied to Actualize on a whim (which for me includes extensive research), without actually thinking I’d follow through.
Six months laters, I’m now a full-stack developer who cannot believe it took me an additional decade plus from my first attempt to code at 16 to following through and becoming what I always knew I should be.

Actualize.Social

Actualize.Social is a web app and native mobile app. This application is a micro network for Actualize Coding Bootcamp students and graduates. The goal of the application was to allow students and graduates to communicate and ask graduates to informational interviews, while also allowing blogging capabilities.

  • Backend and APIs

    The backend was built using Ruby on Rails and PostgreSQL. I used Twilio for messaging capabilities and MapboxGL for GPS and mapping.

  • Frontend

    The frontend was broken up into a web application and native application. The web app was built using Vuejs and the native app was built with React Native.
